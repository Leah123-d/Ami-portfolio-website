Ami's New England
Clam Chowder

I first tried New England clam chowder during a trip with my family to Boston. On that crisp autumn afternoon, I stumbled into a cozy restaurant and found my favorite soup.
This recipe is my tribute to that memorable first taste and the warmth it brought on a chilly day.

Serving: 5 hearty bowls of soup
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 1 yellow onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 2 ribs of celery
  • 2 large Yukon Gold potatoes (~1 lb)
  • 4 slices of thick-cut bacon
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 3 (6.5 oz.) cans of chopped clams
  • 3 cups of half and half
  • 1 (8 oz.) bottle of clam juice
  • 1/2 cup of flour
  • 1/4 stick of butter
  • Ground black pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Oyster crackers
  • Salt

Reminder:

Storage: Can be refrigerated in an airtight container for 3 days.

Reheating: Thaw before heating. Simmer on medium heat, stirring frequently, until thoroughly heated through.

Prep:

  • Dice onion, mince garlic
  • Slice or dice celery
  • Peel and cube potatoes (~1-inch)
  • Slice bacon into 1-inch strips
  • Drain and SAVE the clam juice from the cans of chopped clams

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Warm the soup pot over medium fire, add a small amount of cooking oil to prevent sticking.
  2. Add the bacon to the pot.
  3. Once cooked, take out and set aside the bacon.
  4. In the remaining bacon grease, cook the diced onion and minced garlic for 2 minutes.
  5. Pour in chicken stock, the bottle of clam juice, and the juice saved from the cans.
  6. Add in the potatoes and celery, simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  7. During this time, slowly add in 3/4 of the flour while stirring continuously.
  8. In the same manner, add the chicken broth and then the half and half.
  9. Add the bay leaves.
  10. Bring the soup to a gentle boil and reduce to a simmer.
  11. If thicker soup is preferred, mix the remaining flour with 2-3 tbsp of soup in a separate bowl and whisk until smooth. Slowly add this mixture to the simmering soup while stirring constantly until the desired consistency.
  12. Simmer for 25 minutes, partially covered.
  13. When potatoes are fork-tender, reduce heat to low and stir in the clams.
  14. Cook for another 5 minutes. Remove the bay leaves and stir in the butter and chopped bacon.
  15. Ladle soup into bowls and serve with pepper and oyster crackers. Season with salt and pepper to taste!
